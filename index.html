Overview

Discovering diverse visual patterns in continuous cellular automata (CA) is a challenging exploration problem due to the vastness and redundancy of high-dimensional behavioral spaces. Traditional methods like novelty search (NS) expand locally by mutating known novel solutions but often plateau when local novelty runs out. We introduce Expedition and Expansion (E&E), a hybrid strategy that interleaves NS-driven local expansion with goal-directed expeditions. During expeditions, a vision language model (VLM) generates linguistic goals that drive search toward unexplored behavioral regions, overcoming local diversity plateaus. We evaluate E&E's ability to explore Flow Lenia, a continuous CA, against three baselines: random search, a genetic algorithm, and NS. Our results show that E&E consistently discovers more diverse visual patterns, with gains transferring to independent embedding spaces. Genealogy analysis reveals that expedition-discovered solutions produce disproportionately many descendants, suggesting that they unlock new regions that drive long-term exploration. Finally, we discuss the broader potential of E&E for navigating complex search spaces in other artificial life and artificial intelligence systems.

Key Contributions

  • Combining novelty search and autotelic (creating its own goals) exploration.
  • Using Vision language models as goal selection agents to explore a complex system.
  • Using foundational models for the optimizaiton of complex system dynamic toward language based goals.
